Restaurant Safety App — UX Case Study

A mobile app concept designed to help users with dietary restrictions quickly determine whether a restaurant is safe for them — reducing anxiety, decision fatigue, and social friction.

Research Insights

Key Pain Points
  • Trust gap: Dietary labels are vague, inconsistent, and rarely address cross-contamination.
  • Decision fatigue: Filtering through uncertain options creates stress and slows down decision-making.
  • Social friction: Group dining can feel like a negotiation, making users less likely to advocate for themselves.
User Persona: Melinda (24)

A young professional with multiple dietary restrictions who wants to confidently choose restaurants without extra verification steps or feeling like a burden in group settings.

Key Attributes

  1. Needs clear, detailed information about dietary safety (not just menu labels).
  2. Experiences anxiety around cross-contamination and incomplete information.
  3. Values efficiency and confidence when making dining decisions.
  4. Often balances personal safety with social expectations in group settings.

Goals

  1. Quickly rule out restaurants that feel unsafe or uncertain.
  2. Feel confident in a decision without needing extra research or calls.
  3. Participate in group dining without feeling like a burden.

Frustrations

  1. Vague claims like “we can accommodate” without concrete details.
  2. Having to do extra research or make phone calls to feel safe.
  3. Feeling socially uncomfortable advocating for dietary needs.
Problem Statement

Users with dietary restrictions need an efficient and trustworthy way to assess restaurant safety and suitability so they can make confident dining decisions without unnecessary anxiety or social friction.

Low-Fidelity Search Results Design

The search results page prioritizes scannability and visual clarity. Sorting controls are placed at the top, and dietary safety is visually distinguished between fully safe and partially accommodating restaurants.

Interaction Design — Decision Flow

Designed a complete micro-interaction from entry → safety evaluation → decision. The flow emphasizes reducing uncertainty and supporting confident action.

Design Principles Applied

  • Visual hierarchy: Safety information is prioritized over aesthetics.
  • Anxiety reduction: Clear verdict language replaces vague “may accommodate” phrasing.
  • Scannability: Card-based layout allows quick comparison.
  • Decision support: “Safe for you” vs “Did not meet needs” reduces ambiguity.
